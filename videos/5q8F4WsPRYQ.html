Tesla's true intentions became apparent as construction progressed and his private communications revealed plans far more ambitious than Morgan had anticipated. Tesla intended to provide free electrical power to anyone with appropriate receiving equipment, eliminating the need for power companies, fuel consumption, and centralized energy distribution systems.

When Morgan learned that Tesla planned to give away electrical power rather than sell communication services, the financial relationship deteriorated rapidly. Morgan reportedly asked, "If anyone can draw on the power, where do we put the meter?" The question highlighted the fundamental incompatibility between Tesla's vision of abundant free energy and the business models that required scarcity and consumption to generate profits. Funding was abruptly withdrawn in 1906, halting construction before the system could become operational. Tesla desperately sought alternative investors, but found no one willing to finance technology that would eliminate rather than create revenue opportunities. The incomplete tower stood abandoned as Tesla struggled to complete his work through personal resources and small private donations.

By 1917, the tower was demolished for scrap metal to satisfy Tesla's mounting debts. The property was foreclosed and sold, erasing the physical evidence of humanity's most advanced attempt at wireless power transmission. Tesla's detailed research notes and technical specifications disappeared with many documents reportedly seized by government agents following his death in 1943. The suppression of Tesla's wireless power system represented more than the failure of a single invention. It marked the moment when humanity chose energy scarcity over energy abundance, corporate control over technological freedom.

The world that emerged from this decision was shaped by artificial limitations rather than technical possibilities. Modern wireless power research remains constrained by efficiency concerns and safety regulations that prevent the large-scale implementation Tesla had envisioned. No contemporary technology approaches the global scope or revolutionary implications of the Warden Cliff system, suggesting that Tesla's work was not merely ahead of its time, but actively prevented from reaching completion. The destruction of wireless power transmission technology ensured that energy would remain a commodity to be bought and sold rather than a natural resource freely available to all humanity. Tesla's vision of a world powered by the earth itself was buried beneath the economic imperatives of industries built on scarcity and control.

These 10 cases reveal more than individual technological failures or corporate conspiracies. They expose a systematic pattern where breakthrough innovations are evaluated not by their potential to improve human civilization, but by their threat to existing power structures and profit models. Each invention was suppressed not because it didn't work, but because it worked too well, promising to eliminate the artificial scarcity upon which entire industries depend. The future we inhabit is not the future we could have chosen, but the one we were allowed to have.
