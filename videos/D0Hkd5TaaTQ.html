Scalers are physical quantities that only have a magnitude but no direction. And remember, magnitude is just another way of saying size. And so it can be measured with a numerical value. For example, if a car travels at 22 m/s, 22 would be the magnitude of the speed.

Other scalar quantities include things like distance, mass, temperature, and time, although there are loads more. Vectors on the other hand have both a magnitude and a direction.
