The Observer Effect in Quantum Physics

Transcript

In the world of quantum physics, the observer effect reveals something mind-bending. The act of observation alters what is being observed. This theory originated from experiments like the double slit experiment where particles behave differently when observed compared to when they were not. It implies that consciousness plays a role in shaping reality itself, challenging our definitions of existence and observation. This curious concept extends beyond science.

It inspires reflections in philosophy, art, and even psychology, reminding us that our perceptions exist uniquely as we engage with them. As viewers, this can lead to profound questions. How does your perception influence your reality? Can awareness change outcomes?
