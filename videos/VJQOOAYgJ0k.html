The scariest Nazi project wasn't a weapon. It was a bell. They called it D Glock. And witnesses said it glowed with a strange violet light. Scientists claimed it needed a mysterious liquid called Xerum 525.

And those who worked on it reportedly died from radiation-like burns. Some believe it wasn't just a weapon, but a device to bend space and time. With this technology, Hitler's army would be unstoppable. They could crush entire cities, move troops instantly, and unleash weapons the world had never seen. Although, when Germany surrendered in 1945, the Bell, its scientists, and the secret facility all vanished.

Only fragmented documents remained, fueling the idea that the machine didn't just fail. It might have worked.
