What Is the Observer Effect?

Transcript

In quantum mechanics, the act of observation changes the outcome. Take a particle. Until it's observed, it exists in a cloud of probabilities. Once measured, it collapses into a single state, but only because someone looked. The observer effect challenges the idea that reality exists independently of us.
